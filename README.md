# Parkinson's Disease Detection using Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project was developed as part of an internship study. The primary objective is to accurately detect the presence of Parkinson's Disease (PD) in subjects based on a specific set of biomedical voice measurements and clinical symptoms. 

Due to the sensitive nature of medical data, real-world datasets for Parkinson's are often small or highly imbalanced. To overcome this, this study leverages **Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)** to synthesize artificial patient data, allowing us to train a more robust and accurate discriminative model.

## 🧠 Methodology: The GAN Approach
Instead of relying solely on traditional machine learning classifiers, this project utilizes the architecture of a GAN:
1. **The Generator:** Learns the underlying distribution of the actual Parkinson's disease data to generate highly realistic, synthetic patient records.
2. **The Discriminator:** Trained to distinguish between the "true" clinical conditions of actual Parkinson's patients and the "synthetic" data generated by the model. 

By pushing the generator and discriminator to compete, the discriminator becomes exceptionally skilled at identifying the nuanced symptom patterns of Parkinson's Disease, which is ultimately utilized for our primary classification task.
